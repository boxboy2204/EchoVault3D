# Games Ideas

## 1) Neon Courier (Arcade Delivery Roguelite)
- **Pitch:** You are a bike courier in a flooded cyberpunk city where routes and hazards reshuffle every night.
- **Core loop:** Accept risky deliveries -> plan route through dynamic districts -> dodge gangs/weather/traffic -> upgrade bike and gadgets.
- **Hook:** Time pressure plus route strategy; every shortcut is dangerous.
- **Player fantasy:** "I am the fastest rider in a hostile city."
- **Session length:** 15-25 minute runs.
- **Run structure:**
  1. Pick 2-3 delivery contracts with different risk/reward.
  2. Plot route on city map with predicted hazards.
  3. Execute run in real-time traversal segments.
  4. Cash out, repair bike, buy one permanent unlock.
- **Core systems:**
  - District modifiers: flood depth, police heat, gang activity, visibility.
  - Bike handling model: acceleration, drift control, hydroplane risk.
  - Package traits: fragile, perishable, illegal, decoy.
  - Upgrade trees: speed, survivability, utility (EMP horn, grappling hook, waterproof cargo).
- **Meta progression:**
  - Reputation with factions unlocks contracts and map intel.
  - Garage upgrades persist across runs.
  - Cosmetic bike/streetwear rewards for challenge milestones.
- **Difficulty model:**
  - Tension comes from stacked constraints (time + weather + heat).
  - Failure should still grant partial currency for momentum.
- **MVP scope (first playable):**
  - 1 city map, 3 districts, 6 contract types, 8 upgrades, 2 rival gang AI archetypes.
  - Goal: prove movement feel and route-planning tension.
- **Big risks:**
  - Traversal must feel great immediately.
  - Route strategy UI could become too complex if overloaded.

## 2) Graveyard Shift (Cozy Horror Management)
- **Pitch:** Run a tiny midnight diner that serves humans and monsters without letting either side discover the other.
- **Core loop:** Seat customers strategically -> cook mixed menus -> solve social conflicts -> expand diner and hire unusual staff.
- **Hook:** Cozy systems with stealthy social puzzle tension.

## 3) Signal Lost (Single-Player Extraction Survival)
- **Pitch:** Crash-landed on an alien moon, you launch short extraction runs from a fragile base while storms erase paths.
- **Core loop:** Prep loadout -> raid zones for fuel/parts -> escape before storm wall -> craft permanent base upgrades.
- **Hook:** Extraction pacing without PvP pressure.

## 4) Tiny Rebellion (Tactics + City Builder)
- **Pitch:** Lead a small resistance in a city occupied by clockwork enforcers.
- **Core loop:** Build hidden safehouses by day -> conduct turn-based sabotage missions by night -> trigger district uprisings.
- **Hook:** Missions directly reshape city-builder map control.
- **Player fantasy:** "I turn a powerless neighborhood into a coordinated uprising."
- **Session length:** 30-45 minute chapters (day + night cycle).
- **Chapter structure:**
  1. Day phase: allocate workers, hide resources, expand influence.
  2. Intel phase: scan patrol routes, identify weak infrastructure.
  3. Night mission: deploy squad in turn-based tactical map.
  4. Aftermath: occupation adapts, civilians react, district stats update.
- **Core systems:**
  - City layer: morale, surveillance, supply lines, propaganda pressure.
  - Tactical layer: stealth cones, noise, synchronized actions, non-lethal vs lethal choices.
  - Heat system: high-profile actions increase future mission difficulty.
  - District control: ownership shifts unlock passive bonuses and mission types.
- **Progression:**
  - Recruit specialists (hacker, medic, saboteur, agitator).
  - Unlock doctrine cards that modify strategy each chapter.
  - Narrative events branch based on your methods.
- **Design pillars:**
  - Every tactical win should visibly change the city map.
  - Civilian trust is as important as military success.
  - Resource scarcity forces meaningful tradeoffs.
- **MVP scope (first playable):**
  - 2 districts, 1 occupation faction, 4 mission templates, 5 recruit classes.
  - Goal: validate city-to-mission feedback loop.
- **Big risks:**
  - Balancing two genres can create interface overload.
  - Campaign snowballing may reduce challenge if not tuned carefully.

## 5) Last Lightkeeper (Narrative Puzzle Adventure)
- **Pitch:** Maintain a chain of ancient lighthouses across floating islands as the sea level rises.
- **Core loop:** Sail to towers -> repair light mechanisms -> decode old keeper logs -> choose who gets evacuation routes.
- **Hook:** Mechanical puzzles tied to moral choices.

## 6) Rumble Garden (Creature Battler + Farming)
- **Pitch:** Grow hybrid plant-creatures and enter underground arena circuits.
- **Core loop:** Plant traits -> crossbreed creatures -> train abilities through minigames -> fight in short tactical matches.
- **Hook:** Breeding systems create surprising combat builds.

## 7) Deep Freeze Inc. (Co-op Disaster Logistics)
- **Pitch:** 1-4 players run emergency supply chains during an endless winter.
- **Core loop:** Scout weather windows -> route convoys -> defend depots -> prioritize towns to keep alive.
- **Hook:** Logistics decisions are the combat.

## 8) Threadrunner (Parkour Rhythm Platformer)
- **Pitch:** Sprint across collapsing megastructures where movement speed controls the soundtrack layers.
- **Core loop:** Chain wall-runs/slides/grapples -> stay on beat for score multipliers -> unlock traversal mods.
- **Hook:** Rhythm is generated by your movement, not fixed tracks.

## 9) Jury of One (Investigation Sim)
- **Pitch:** As the only judge in a post-collapse settlement, you investigate crimes and issue rulings that change society.
- **Core loop:** Gather testimony/evidence -> detect contradictions -> issue verdicts -> watch long-term social effects.
- **Hook:** No "right" ending; law evolves from your patterns.

## 10) Drift Kingdoms (Action RTS Lite)
- **Pitch:** Command flying islands in real-time, physically ramming and docking to steal resources.
- **Core loop:** Harvest sky currents -> build defenses -> board enemy islands -> relocate before storms.
- **Hook:** Territory is mobile and collisions are a weapon.

## Shortlist Candidates (Highest Potential)
- **Neon Courier:** Strong replayability and clear arcade appeal.
- **Graveyard Shift:** Broad streamer-friendly personality.
- **Tiny Rebellion:** High depth for strategy players.
- **Threadrunner:** Distinct identity and movement-first design.
